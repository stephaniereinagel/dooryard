# Seasonal focus — by month (NW Arkansas)

> Extracted from `homestead/operations/assistant/backlogs.md`. Use this as a reminder of what's timely right now — individual garden tasks come from MyGardenCoach.

## 🌱 April
- Heavy garden focus: transplants going in, final frost date usually early-mid April
- Succession sow: lettuce, radishes, carrots, beets every 2 weeks
- Start warm-season transplants (tomato, pepper, squash) when soil warm
- Daffodils still available for stand; tulips peaking
- Chick/duckling watch if any brooding or new birds
- Early stand lineup: eggs, cookies (chocolate chip + royal-icing Easter cookies until Easter), granola, daffodils
- Cut + hang any flowering herbs for drying

## 🌾 May
- Tomatoes/peppers/cukes in ground
- First lettuce/radish harvest
- Stand adds: bouquets (tulips early, iris), first greens, radishes
- Hen broodiness watch
- Mosquito mitigation starts (standing water audit)

## ☀️ June–July
- Main garden maintenance: weeds + water + pest pressure peaks
- Succession: beans, carrots, cucumbers
- Stand peak: summer vegetables, zinnias, sunflowers, preserved goods
- Egg production may dip in extreme heat — ensure shade + electrolytes
- Baking schedule around heat of day

## 🌻 August
- Tomato peak → preserves, sauce batches
- Fall garden planning: start brassicas indoors for transplant
- Flock: heat stress watch, shade, cold water refresh 2x daily if possible
- Stand: late summer produce, dried herb bundles, flower bouquets

## 🍂 September–October
- Fall garden (greens, roots, brassicas) goes in
- Stand rotates to: fall bouquets, last tomatoes, sweet peppers, squash, eggs, baked goods
- Stand season ends last Friday of October
- Prep cold-hardy beds, mulch heavy for winter

## 🪵 November–December
- Stand closed. Friday becomes property/projects day.
- Coop prep for cold: deep-bedding method, draft check, heated waterers
- Inventory: assess seed storage, order spring seeds
- Fencing repairs + tool maintenance (big chunks of time)
- Garden: cover crops / mulch heavy / garlic planting
- Baking: holiday gift boxes if we do them

## ❄️ January–February
- Indoor seed starting late Jan / early Feb
- Coop + duck house deep clean
- Major property/infrastructure projects — prime time
- Tool sharpening, repair backlog
- Plan next season stand lineup

## 🌷 March
- Stand season opens (mid-March)
- Harden-off starts for transplants
- Spring cleaning at stand area, fresh signage
